Habitat Abstraction Layer (2017 - 2020)

A dynamic control system for Featherston House.


  • An architecturally award winning house designed by prominent Australian Robin Boyd in the late 1960s.

  • An intuitive climate control system that forecasts the weather, and then prepares the building for various conditions based on modern adaptive comfort modelling, similar to ASHRAE-55 and EN-15251.

  • Comprising of over a dozen temperature and humidity sensors, plus solar and wind read across a series of ADAM-6017s, scraped forecast data, and then a series of ADAM-6060s, plus some vendor-specific controllers for HVAC, Solar shades, Destratification fans etc.


  • Published in ArchDaily, the most visited architecture website worldwide, and The Design Files, Australia‚Äôs most popular design blog.

  • Utilising Java/Jython, Python, InfluxDB/Grafana, HTML/CSS & Javascript.

Ticker (2019)

A capacitance-sensor giving scale to the microbiome.
